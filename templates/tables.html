Prediction Model Explanation

The Margin, Total Points, Points For and Points Allowed Residual Scatter Plot Charts shows the difference between the actual and project stats per game. The Turnover Margin vs Actual and Projected Margin Chart shows the Turnovers in relation to our Predicted Model and the Actual result.

Accuracy of Prediction Model

The Margin, Total points, Point For and Points Allowed were predicted based on the 3rd down percentage, Time of possession, first downs, home/away games, passing yards, penalty yards, plays, rushing yards, sacked/sacks, takeaways, total_yards and turnovers data of the teams using the OLS Regression model of the StatsModels. The coefficients and the intercepts were calculated based on these variables and the data was predicted for each of the teams. The plot below shows the difference in the Actual value and the Predicted value using our calculations using Plotly in Python. As seen in the plots, the predicted data was quite close to the zero line. The yearly averages for the predicted versus actual data also was spot on on quite a few values.

Prediction Model Explanation

This sankey (alluvial) diagram illustrates an elo scoring system for the entirety of the 2016 NFL season. Predicted win %, elo scoring based on h2h matchups/locales are aggregated (on average) by Conference, Division, and Team tiers. The standard score for teams is 1500, higher scores indicate an improved probability of victory, and scores lower than the standard suggest the chances of a loss are greater. Unexpected results (i.e. win when predicted loss) result in a greater overall score for the following week's game and vice versa.
